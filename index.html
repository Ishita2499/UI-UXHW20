Overview

About Chess'n Math

The Chess and Math Association (CMA) is a non-profit organization which promotes chess in schools in Canada at an affordable price. Chess helps children develop logical thinking, self-confidence and concentration skills in a structured and fun environment.

The Problem

We identified that, the Chess'n Math organizations website has an extremely high bounce rate, due to a myriad of reasons. Along with the competition, the following factors are the reasons for the difficulties. 1. Confusing Navigation & Homepage Design 2. Disorganized content & links 3. Extremely long checkout process

The Solution

Instead of redesigning the entire website, we will first focus on improving the homepage, course finder, the checkout and registration pages. We believe, that by redesigning the Chess'n Math website with a focus on creating an organized and seamless navigation, we will increase the number of registrations.

User Research


Research Methods Used

Before we dived into redesigning the website, we had to conduct qualitative & quantitative research.For our research, we looked at both the users and stakeholders. We conducted:

5 User Interviews:

15-20 Minutes each, asking for their motivations for lessons, and their experience with booking classes.

Stakeholder Interview:

Learning what they expected from the re-design and what they wanted for the future.

Survey:

Getting quantitative data about their experiences, and asking for more feedback for improvement.

In both the interview and the survey, we asked questions focused around, the users experience navigating through the website and what changes they would suggest be made to the website. As for our stakeholders, we asked what questions around what they wanted to achieve from the re-design and which existing elements of the website worked well in their opinion.

What we learned from our Empathy Map?

We learned that, Chess'n Math has a credible, established clientele and has partnerships with many schools. They have information on their website that is easy to understand. However, the website's main pain points are: excessive amounts of information, a disorganized interface and lack of cohesiveness.

Usability Testing

I tested my prototype on 7 different users.

Objective:

A user would like to register for one term of Friday 4:30pm chess lessons.

Tasks
  1. You're looking for some online lessons for chess for your child, and want try just registering for one session with Chess 'n Math Association. You want to look for all the lessons that are available for beginners.
  2. Now that you've seen all the beginner online class options, you're looking to get a lesson for Monday 4:30pm. Please register for the class.
  3. You are satisfied with the chess lessons, and are looking to make an account so that registration will be much faster in the future! Create an account, and a profile in your account for your child.
Feedback:
  • User was able to find the lessons through the nav bar that was created. They were able to locate the class and add it to cart. One user said that there was no indication that the item was added to cart.
  • Also another user suggested that it would be helpful to have something that showed that items were added to cart.
  • Finally, a user mentioned that some of the images and white space on the homepage could be cleaned up.

FINAL TAKEAWAYS

This is just phase one of the overall rebrand journey for the Chess'n Math organization.

The next steps would involve making iteration to the current prototypes to further improve the overall design & flow. We will also work on adding the additional elements highlighted below and work on completing the entire web redesign.

  1. ADD A FILTER: So that people can find certain classes that fit their needs easier.

  2. AUTONOMY: Have the ability to break off from the major website so that you know you're only dealing with Toronto.

  3. ADD MORE IMAGES: Be able to add more interactive images and videos for the website to make it more engaging.

  4. FINISH IT OFF: Be able to build out the rest of the website pages!
